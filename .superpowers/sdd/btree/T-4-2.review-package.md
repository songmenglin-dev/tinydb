### Deviation B — 1-byte node-type tag at offset 0
The brief's locked layouts had `next_leaf_pid` (leaf) and `key_count` (internal) both starting at offset 0, with no discriminator. Implementation adds a u8 node-type byte at offset 0:
- `0x00` = uninitialized (all zeros — backward-compat with T-4.1 on-disk state)
- `0x01` = leaf
- `0x02` = internal

All other fields shift by one byte:
- **Leaf** (12-byte header): `offset 0 u8 type | offset 4 u32 next_leaf_pid | offset 8 u16 key_count | offset 10 u16 reserved | offset 12 payload`
- **Internal** (12-byte header): `offset 0 u8 type | offset 4 u16 key_count | offset 6 u16 reserved | offset 8 u32 first_child_pid | offset 12 payload`

Reviewer question: Is the discriminator the only way to distinguish leaf vs internal on disk? Are both readers (back-compat with `0x00`, forward with `0x01`/`0x02`) correct?

### Deviation C — `BTreeOverflowError` lives in `tinydb/errors.py`
The brief literally said "Define `BTreeOverflowError(TinydbError)` in `src/tinydb/index/btree.py`". But `btree_leaf.py` and `btree_internal.py` need to raise it (they raise, but they must not import from `btree.py` which imports them — circular). Implementation moved it to `errors.py` and re-exports from `tinydb.index`.

Public surface unchanged: `from tinydb.index import BTreeOverflowError` works.

Reviewer question: Is the re-export correct in both `tinydb/errors.py/__all__` and `tinydb/index/__init__.py`? Is the class shape right (inherits TinydbError, has docstring)?
